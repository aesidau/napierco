Ms. Jenny Going

Conductor

Jenny Going is Musical Director of the Essendon Symphony where she has been conductor since 2008. She is currently completing a Masters of Music degree at The University of Melbourne under Benjamin Northey, specialising in orchestral conducting. In 2015 she was honoured to be the inaugural recipient of the Rosemary and John Hopkins Award.

Jenny started her musical career as a trombonist with the Royal New Zealand Navy Band then went on to study jazz trombone and conducting at the University of the Arts in Philadelphia, USA. During her time in America time Jenny toured extensively throughout the west coast of the USA and recorded with Ska Band 'Ruder Than You'.

She has worked as conductor for Bendigo Music Festival Week, The Waikato Youth Orchestra, been Musical Director of the ZHFM Hamilton Big Band and for the Hamilton Operatic Society. Jenny has been Head of Music of school on both sides of the Tasman most recently as Music Curriculum Coordinator at Princes Hill Secondary College in Carlton, Melbourne. She is a regular presenter at national music education conferences throughout Australia. As Managing Director and founder of Noteable - Music Education Resources, she has written and published resources for classroom music teachers.

For further details, see www.jennygoing.com.au.

History

The orchestra was founded in the 1990's by Paul Green (who still plays with us) as a string ensemble. Over the years the orchestra has grown to be a full symphony orchestra.

During this time, the orchestra performed under several names including Napier Chamber Orchestra, Napier Concert Orchestra and Napier Community Orchestra. However, in 2012 it established itself as the Essendon Symphony.

Previous conductors have included:

  • Paul Fitzsimon
  • Chris van Tuinen
  • Mark Sheill

The Essendon Symphony is the only community-based orchestra in Melbourne's western suburbs. We are often looking for new players especially for violin, viola, double bass and brass.
