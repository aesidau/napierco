Ms. Jenny Going

Conductor

The present conductor of the Essendon Symphony is Jenny Going, who comes to us having joined the brain drain from New Zealand to Australia in 2006. Jenny is currently curriculum coordinator for music at Princes Hill Secondary College. She is also managing director of Noteable - Music Education Resources. Jenny started her musical career as a trombonist with the NZ Navy Band. After leaving the Navy and obtaining a Psych degree (which has come in very useful for a lifetime with musicians), Jenny studied jazz trombone and conducting at the University of the Arts in Philadelphia. She has worked on both sides of the Tasman and has performed with, and conducted many groups from big bands, orchestras and concert bands and been musical director for a number of musical theatre productions.

History

The orchestra was founded in the 1990's by Paul Green (who still plays with us) as a string ensemble. Over the years the orchestra has grown to be a full symphony orchestra.

During this time, the orchestra performed under several names including Napier Chamber Orchestra, Napier Concert Orchestra and Napier Community Orchestra. However, in 2012 it established itself as the Essendon Symphony.

Previous conductors have included:

  • Paul Fitzsimon
  • Chris van Tuinen
  • Mark Sheill

The Essendon Symphony is the only community-based orchestra in Melbourne's western suburbs. We are often looking for new players especially for violin, viola, double bass and brass.
