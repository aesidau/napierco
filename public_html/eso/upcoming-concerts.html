Next concert: Sunday Romance (Winter Music in the Valley)

Sunday 6th July, 3pm - FREE!

Clocktower Centre, 750 Mt Alexander Rd, Moonee Ponds VIC 3039

This is the fourth year of Moonee Valley's Winter Music in the Valley music series. This year, the Essendon Symphony will bring the music series to a close with a memorable concert at the Clocktower Centre featuring the stunning Bruch Violin Concerto. We are thrilled to have Cameron Jamieson join us as the violin soloist for that piece.

Cameron Jamieson is celebrating his 20th year playing the violin and his 5th year with Charlotte, his John Simmers instrument from his home town of Brisbane. In 2011, Cameron graduated from the Queensland Conservatorium of Music with a Bachelor of Music in Advanced Violin Performance completing extensive studies with Michele Walsh. In 2012, Cameron moved to Melbourne to further his studies at the Australian National Academy of Music, with William Hennessy, and to work with the Melbourne Symphony Orchestra. His highlights include the MSO's re-opening of Hamer Hall with Mahler's Third symphony, premiering Paul Dean's Clarinet Quintet for Dame Elizabeth Murdoch, directing the ANAM orchestra for a piano concerto with Paavali Jumppanen, and the opening night of Quartetthaus.

Pieces include:

  • Bruch - Violin Concerto
  • Borodin - Prince Igor Overture

Further details on the full Winter Music in the Valley concert series (Sundays 15th June - 6th July) are available on the City of Moonee Valley website.
